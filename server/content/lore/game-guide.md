## A night of play

A session moves through a familiar shape. The real guide will give the timings
and the etiquette; for now, the outline is enough to test the layout.

1. Arrive, sign in, and check the notice board.
2. Play out the evening in character.
3. Note anything you want to carry into your downtime.
4. Submit your downtime before the stated deadline.

## What to bring

- A clear sense of what your character *wants* this month.
- Any props or costume that help you hold the role.
- Patience: the Storyteller moves around the room and reaches everyone in turn.

## Downtime in brief

Between sessions you submit a downtime describing what your character does with
their nights. Feeding is always declared and validated rather than invented for
you, and a project action is resolved and rolled on its own. The full mechanics
live in the **[rules](rules)** page.

A note on tone:

> Play to lose well. The best scenes come from a character who can be hurt.

## A worked example of a pool

Most rolls are an Attribute plus a Skill. A specialisation that fits the action
adds a die and grants the 9-again quality:

```
Persuade a doorman:  Presence + Persuasion
With a fitting spec:  Presence + Persuasion + 1 die, 9-again
```

Inline, that same idea reads as a `Presence + Persuasion` pool.
