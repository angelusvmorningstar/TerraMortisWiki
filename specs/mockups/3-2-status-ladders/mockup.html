Status

Design-lock mockup for story 3-2 — static HTML, no live data, two states shown below. City Status is always fully public — every logged-in viewer sees every character, no gating, matching TM Suite's own rule exactly. Covenant and Clan Status are gated: a viewer only sees the ladder for their own character's covenant(s) (primary plus any covenant they hold standing > 0 in) and their own clan — never another faction's internal ranking. The subheading under each ladder names whose standing is shown, in words, so the gating reads as intentional rather than an incomplete page. Dot indicators use TM Suite's own .pointed / .pointed.hollow convention, ported fresh into this mockup — it does NOT exist in this repo's components.css yet, this story is the first thing here that needs it. Accordion shell reuses .roster-section from story 3-1 rather than a tab or carousel switcher — this site already uses accordions everywhere else, a second switching paradigm on one page would be inconsistent for no real gain (nobody is on a phone mid-scene needing a swipe gesture the way TM Suite's live play tool is).
